Kazakhstan declared independence from the Soviet Union on December 16, 1991.
The Kazakh Khanate was founded in 1465 by Janibek Khan and Kerey Khan.
Toktar Aubakirov became the first Kazakh cosmonaut in 1991 when he flew aboard Soyuz TM-13 to the Mir space station.
The capital of Kazakhstan was moved from Almaty to Astana in 1997.
Kazakhstan joined the United Nations on March 2, 1992.
The Silk Road historically passed through the territory of modern Kazakhstan connecting East and West.
