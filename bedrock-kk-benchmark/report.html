The Understanding-Correctness Gap
Across all 53 models, Understanding averages 18 percentage points higher than Correctness. Models can parse Kazakh far better than they can produce accurate answers in it. This gap widens dramatically in bottom-tier models — they understand fragments but hallucinate answers.
GLM-5's Understanding Edge
GLM-5 and LLaMA 4 Scout both scored 196/200 on Understanding — matching or exceeding Claude. The differentiator was Kazakh output quality and factual accuracy, where Claude maintained its lead. Chinese models' Kazakh comprehension benefits from shared Turkic linguistic features.

Key Findings & Insights
🌟
Surprise: GLM-5 Ties Claude Sonnet
Z.AI's GLM-5 (Chinese company) scored 91.3%, tying with Claude Sonnet 4.6. A Chinese model matching Anthropic's on Kazakh suggests that Turkic language knowledge transfers from Chinese training corpora that include Central Asian content.
⚡
Size Isn't Everything
LLaMA 4 Scout 17B (89.7%) beats many models 10x its size. Mistral's 675B flagship barely edges DeepSeek V3.2 (89.3% vs 88.0%). Architecture and training data quality matter more than raw parameter count for low-resource languages.
📚
Linguistics is the Litmus Test
Average score of just 12.0/30 on linguistics questions (phonetics, morphophonemic rules). This category alone separates models with genuine Kazakh competence from those relying on surface-level pattern matching.
🔄
Translation Asymmetry: 57% Harder
EN→KK translation (16.2/30) is dramatically harder than KK→EN (25.5/30). Generating correct Kazakh requires mastery of agglutinative morphology, vowel harmony, and case suffixing that comprehension-only tasks don't demand.
🎭
Culture as Differentiator
Cultural knowledge (13.4/30) and deep cultural understanding (15.4/30) are key separators. Models that rank Gold on grammar but fail culture reveal a gap between language fluency and cultural competence — a critical distinction for real-world deployment.
⚠
DeepSeek R1: Asterisk Required
DeepSeek R1 scored 90.0% but only answered 3 of 20 questions (17 errors, likely rate limits). Its per-question quality is exceptional, but the sample size is too small for a definitive ranking. Included with a caveat.
❌ NVIDIA Nemotron: Consistent Failure
All 4 NVIDIA Nemotron models scored below 54%, with nano variants under 20%. Despite large parameter counts (up to 120B), these models lack meaningful Kazakh language data in their training mix. Provider average: 29.7%.
🔒 20 Models Inaccessible
20 additional models on Bedrock were not testable — they require explicit enablement in the AWS console. This includes several Google, Cohere, and Stability AI models that may have ranked well.
📊 Scout 17B > Maverick 17B
Among same-generation LLaMA 4 models, Scout outperforms Maverick by 4.5 percentage points (89.7% vs 85.2%). Both are 17B parameter MoE architectures, but Scout's expert routing appears better suited to low-resource language tasks.
